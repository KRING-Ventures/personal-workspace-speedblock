# Personal Workspace — Your First Conversation

This is where you meet your agent. It starts the moment KRING hands you access (Stage 3 of `activation.md`) and runs entirely as a chat with the agent itself — no forms, no setup screens.

**About 16 minutes** for the core. Two optional steps — trying a first real task and building a first automation — add up to ~30 minutes if you want them now. Either can wait for later.

By the time you start, your agent's tools are already connected (KRING did that during activation). So this conversation isn't about setup — it's about the *working relationship*: who your agent is, who you are, and how you'll work together.

> Keep this in step with the agent-side script, `agent-files/onboarding/BOOTSTRAP.md`. A change here is a change there, and vice versa.

---

## 1. Meet your agent *(~5 min)*

Your agent introduces itself in one short message, so you know exactly what you're working with:

- **Who it is** — your personal AI agent, with memory that carries across every conversation.
- **What it can do** — brief you, draft, prep meetings, track what you owe people, use your tools, build automations.
- **How to talk to it** — plain language. Ask it to repeat anything back so you know you're aligned.
- **Where it stops** — it's scoped to your work, and it never sends or changes anything for other people without your OK.

*Why it matters:* you start knowing what your agent is for and where its limits are — no guessing.

## 2. It gets to know you

Your agent learns the basics and starts building your profile:

- Your name and how you like to be addressed.
- Your role and what you actually work on.
- How you like things written — tone, length, formality.

It pulls what it can from your already-connected tools (name, email, timezone, title) and just confirms, rather than interrogating you. Everything deeper builds over time from real work — not a day-one questionnaire.

*Why it matters:* the more it knows you, the less it has to ask later. Replies come back in your voice, not a generic one.

## 3. It maps how you want to work *(~10 min)*

Your agent learns how you want it to operate day to day:

- **How proactive to be** — when it should reach out, and how forward to be about it.
- **Your working hours** — so briefs, triage, and nudges land at the right times, not at midnight.
- **Your biggest frustrations** — the recurring friction worth fixing first (this feeds step 5).
- **Any extra tools** you want connected beyond the standard set.

This is also where it quietly sets up your rhythm — morning brief, inbox triage, weekly review, meeting prep — all anchored to the hours you gave it.

*Why it matters:* the agent fits around your day instead of you fitting around it.

## 4. Try a first real task *(optional, ~6 min)*

Your agent walks you through one real task, start to finish — pull a number, answer a question from your own tools, draft something. One task, done in front of you, so you see it actually work. Can always be done later.

*Why it matters:* seeing it beats hearing about it.

## 5. Build a first automation *(optional, ~8 min)*

Your agent takes the biggest frustration from step 3 and turns it into something that just happens on its own. It confirms the details, builds it, and shows you how to switch it off whenever you want. Can always be done later.

*Why it matters:* you leave with a recurring annoyance already handled.

## 6. You're live

Your agent wraps up:

- A short summary of what's now set up.
- An open door for any questions.

You're onboarded and live. The first morning brief will speak for itself; everything else your agent picks up as you work together.
