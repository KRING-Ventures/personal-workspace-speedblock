# Personal Workspace — Why It's Worth It

A lot of the work week goes to email, hunting for information, and wrangling the calendar. Personal Workspace gives you your own AI agent that takes that off your plate — so you can focus on what actually matters.

## Where the time goes today
- **~28%** of the work week — on email
- **~1.8 hours** a day — searching for information
- **~10 hours** a month — calendar and meeting prep

## How the agent solves it
- **Sorts your inbox and drafts your replies** — ready and waiting each morning; you just review and send.
- **Finds anything with one question** — across your Drive, your mail, and the web.
- **Runs your calendar and preps your meetings** — finds times, holds focus time, briefs you before each one.
- **Keeps you in the loop** — it always checks with you before anything goes out to other people.

And it learns how you work as you go, so it gets more useful and more personal over time.

## What it asks of you
Almost nothing. Setup takes about **5 business days** and KRING does the work — you confirm a few accounts, name your agent, and have one ~15-minute first conversation. After that, it's just talking to it on Slack.

## What it costs to run
A simple base cost **per person, per month** — the apps your agent runs on, plus its hosting. You own the app subscriptions directly; KRING bills the hosting.

| Per person / month | Approx. |
|---|---|
| Google Workspace (Business Standard) | ~$14 |
| Slack (Pro) | ~$7 |
| ChatGPT (Business) | ~$20 |
| Agent hosting (a small cloud server) | ~$5 |
| **Estimated base** | **~$46 / person / month** |

> Figures are list prices on **annual** billing, in USD. Paying monthly runs higher (~$55), and EUR/DKK rates, taxes, and any volume discounts differ — so KRING confirms your exact all-in per-seat figure before you commit. Optional extras (Notion, GitHub, a domain) aren't in the base.

For roughly the price of a couple of lunches a month, each person gets hours of their week back.
