# Personal Workspace — Activation

How your venture gets set up on Personal Workspace. The whole thing takes about **5 business days**, and most of the work is ours.

Here's the shape of it:

1. **You provision** — confirm a few accounts and name your agents. *(~1 day, your side)*
2. **KRING sets everything up** — we build and connect each agent to your tools. *(~3–4 days, our side, mostly invisible to you)*
3. **Each person meets their agent** — a ~15-minute first conversation, and they're live. *(per user)*

You'll never have to touch the technical setup. Your part is to get a few accounts in place and tell us what to call the agents — we handle the rest.

> This flow is under active refinement — treat the stage split as a working draft.

---

## Stage 1 — You provision *(~1 day)*

**1. Activate the Speedblock**
In Cosmica, set how many people you want set up (one agent each) and accept the terms and pricing.

**2. Sign the agreement**
KRING sends the invoice and agreement. Once it's signed, we start building.

**3. Name your agents**
Pick a name for each person's agent — one per seat. That name becomes the agent's identity everywhere. Send the list to KRING.

---

## Stage 2 — KRING sets everything up *(~3–4 days)*

This is where we do the heavy lifting. We build each person's agent and connect it to your tools so it's ready to work from day one. You don't manage any of this — your only job is to make sure the accounts exist and to give us the access to wire them in.

**4. Get the required apps in place**
Set up accounts for the apps Personal Workspace needs to run (see **What you need** below). We can't connect an agent to a tool the person doesn't have yet.

**5. Give KRING access**
Grant us the access we need to connect each agent to those apps. We'll tell you exactly what's needed, tool by tool, when we start.

That's it from your side. Behind the scenes we're building each agent and wiring it to email, calendar, files, and your team's Slack.

---

## Stage 3 — Each person meets their agent *(~15 min each)*

**6. We hand over access**
We give each person access to their agent in Slack, with a quick check so they know it's really *their* agent.

**7. The agent takes it from here**
Each person has a short first conversation with their agent — who it is, who they are, how they'll work together. No KRING involvement needed. Full flow in `onboarding.md`.

---

## What you need

Each person needs these three apps before we can finish Stage 2. These are the apps Personal Workspace runs on:

- **Google Workspace** (Gmail, Calendar, Drive, Docs, Meet) — your email, calendar, and files, all in one place. One Workspace account per person; a personal `@gmail.com` won't work.
- **Slack** — where each person talks to their agent, and where the team communicates.
- **ChatGPT** — the AI engine the agents run on.

There are also a few **recommended** apps (Notion, GitHub, and others) that people can connect themselves later. They're optional and not part of activation — see `playbook.md` for the full list.
