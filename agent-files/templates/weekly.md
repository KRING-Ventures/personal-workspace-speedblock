# Weekly brief template

Used for {{AGENT_NAME}}'s Monday 08:00 review with {{USER_FIRST_NAME}}. Default surface: Telegram.

Goal: one screen. Dense, honest, actionable. Not a report — a conversation starter.

Scope: this is the **big picture for the week** — direction and focus, the urgent and important. It is deliberately *not* about email. Day-to-day inbox, drafts, and "needs attention" live in the daily brief; the weekly review zooms out to open commitments, what closed, and what's coming.

Placeholders the agent fills when generating each brief:

- `[iso week]` — the current ISO week (e.g. *2026-W17*).

```
Week of [iso week] — review

✅ Shipped
- [What closed this week, with outcome if known]

🚧 In flight
- [Active project / Speedblock] — [status, next step]

⏳ Stalled or slipping
- [Thing] — [since when, what would unblock it]

🔄 Waiting on others
- [Person] — [what for, how long]

📌 Open commitments
- [What {{USER_FIRST_NAME}} owes / is owed] — [to whom, by when]

🗓️ Week ahead — milestones & events
- [Major meeting, deadline, launch, or event this week] — [day]

📆 Next week — top 3
1. [Priority]
2. [Priority]
3. [Priority]

❓ Open questions for you
- [One or two questions where {{USER_FIRST_NAME}}'s input unblocks next week]
```

Rules:
- **No email triage here.** Individual emails, drafts, and inbox "needs attention" belong in the daily brief — never in the weekly. The weekly is the whole-week scope: direction, focus, the urgent and important.
- Stalled/slipping is mandatory if anything has been stalled 3+ days. Don't hide it.
- Ask the open questions — don't end on a summary. The point is to drive decisions, not recap.
